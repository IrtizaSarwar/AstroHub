Planets

Planets are celestial bodies that orbit a star. They are massive enough for their own gravity to make them round and have cleared their orbital neighborhood of other objects. Planets can be rocky, like Earth and Mars, or gaseous, like Jupiter and Saturn. They vary greatly in size, composition, and temperature. Some planets have moons, rings, or other features. The study of planets helps us understand the formation and evolution of planetary systems, including our own.

  • There are eight planets in our solar system.
  • Planets orbit stars in elliptical paths.
  • Planets can have diverse atmospheres and surface features.
