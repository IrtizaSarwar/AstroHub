Galaxies

Galaxies are vast systems of stars, gas, dust, and dark matter, bound together by gravity. They come in various shapes and sizes, from dwarf galaxies containing a few billion stars to giant ellipticals with trillions. Our own solar system resides in the Milky Way galaxy, a spiral galaxy. Galaxies are not uniformly distributed in the universe; they tend to cluster together in groups and superclusters, separated by vast voids. They play a crucial role in the evolution of the universe, providing the environment for star formation and the distribution of elements.

  • The Milky Way is a spiral galaxy.
  • Galaxies are classified by their shape (spiral, elliptical, irregular).
  • Galaxies can collide and merge with each other.
